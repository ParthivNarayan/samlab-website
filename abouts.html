About SAM Lab

Centre for Ocean, River, Atmosphere & Land Sciences (CORAL), IIT Kharagpur

What we do

We study Earth’s systems using geospatial data, modelling, and analytics— from species distributions and landscape processes to climate variability and ecosystem productivity. Our work blends remote sensing, statistical learning, and mechanistic understanding to support conservation and sustainable development.

How we work

  • Satellite, airborne, and in-situ observations (optical, SAR, LiDAR)
  • Modern ML/DL workflows for classification, regression, and causal discovery
  • Landscape & hydrological modelling; fire spread and risk analytics
  • Carbon accounting and ecosystem service evaluation

Ongoing Projects

  • DST Monsoon–Vegetation Interaction Project (2024–27)
  • MoEFCC National Wetland Assessment Initiative (2024–27)
  • Remote Sensing–based Ecosystem Assessment Frameworks
  • Forest Carbon and Biomass Estimation using Fusion of Optical + SAR Data

Past Projects

  • ISRO Biodiversity Characterisation at Landscape Level
  • Forest Fire Risk Modelling and Early Warning Systems
  • Long-Term Vegetation Dynamics & Phenology using Multi-sensor Time Series
  • National Carbon Pool Assessment (various phases)
